In Japanese there are three writing systems...
1. Hiragana - a syllabary with 46 basic characters, where each represents a sound (usually a consonant paired with a vovel). Hiragana are used for native Japanese words.
2. Katakana - the same set of sounds as hiragana, except these characters are used to represent non-native Japanese words.
3. Kanji - each kanji represents a whole word or concept as opposed to a sound. Many Kanji have multiple readings.
It will be essential going forward for you to memorize Hiragana and katakana to be able to sound out words. Kanji will be introduced slowly over time, later.

The vowel sounds: 
a, sounds like "ah" as in father
i, sounds like "ee" as in see
u, sounds like "oo" as in food
e, sounds like "eh" as in red
o, sound like "oh" as in boat
